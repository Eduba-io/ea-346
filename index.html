01 / The Eduba frame

Most organizations automate the wrong things.

About 60% of what a defense-autonomy company does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a real AI problem. The work is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built.

Applied to Vatn, the map resolves quickly. Bristol production travellers, supplier qualification records, Skelmir and TORSK telemetry archives, and ITAR classification history are database and code problems. CMMC 2.0 evidence routing, DFARS flowdowns, export classification for Hanwha and Airbus touchpoints, and change-control gating across Portsmouth and Bristol are rule-based logic. Cooperative swarm autonomy in denied-environment nav is the 10%.

A context architecture over the first two layers gives the engineering and capture teams their cycles back for the third.
